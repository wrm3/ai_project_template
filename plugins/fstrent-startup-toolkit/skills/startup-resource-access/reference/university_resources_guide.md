# University Resources Guide

## Technology Transfer Offices

**How It Works**:
1. University develops IP
2. University files patents
3. Startup licenses IP
4. University gets equity/royalties

**Typical Terms**:
- Upfront: $0-$25k
- Equity: 2-5%
- Royalties: 2-6% of revenue
- Milestones: Funding, development
- Sponsored research: Often required

**Top Universities**:
- MIT, Stanford, UC Berkeley
- Caltech, Harvard, UCSF
- UT Austin, UW, Johns Hopkins

**Benefits**:
- Access cutting-edge research
- Foundational IP
- Faculty expertise
- Lab access
- Student talent

**Challenges**:
- Long negotiations (6-12 months)
- Restrictive terms
- University bureaucracy

## University Lab Access

**Core Facilities (Fee-for-Service)**:
- Sequencing, imaging, proteomics
- $50-$500/hour
- Training required

**Major Facilities**:
- **Stanford**: Nano facilities, imaging, sequencing
- **MIT**: Microsystems labs, nanostructures
- **UC Berkeley**: QB3 cores, nanofab

**Access Process**:
1. Contact facility manager
2. Complete training
3. Submit project proposal
4. Pay per-use fees

**Costs**:
- Training: $0-$500 (one-time)
- Usage: $50-$500/hour
- Commercial rates: 1.5-2x academic

## Student Talent

**Access Methods**:
- Internship programs
- Capstone projects
- Research assistantships
- Career fairs and hackathons

**Benefits**:
- Lower cost ($15-$40/hour)
- Fresh perspectives
- Cutting-edge skills
- Potential co-founders

**Challenges**:
- Limited availability
- Less experience
- High turnover

**Best Practices**:
- Offer meaningful work
- Flexible scheduling
- Pathway to full-time
- Proper IP agreements

## Research Partnerships

**Sponsored Research**:
- Startup pays university for research
- $100k-$1M per year
- 3-5 year agreements
- IP typically shared

**Clinical Trials**:
- University medical centers
- $500k-$5M+ per trial

**Benefits**:
- World-class researchers
- University facilities
- Credibility
- Publications

**Challenges**:
- Slow timelines
- IP disputes
- High costs
- Bureaucracy
