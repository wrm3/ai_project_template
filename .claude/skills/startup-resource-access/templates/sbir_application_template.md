# SBIR Phase I Application Template

## Project Summary (30 lines max)
**Project Title**: [Catchy, descriptive title]

**Company**: [Your company name]

**Innovation**: [What's new and different?]

**Impact**: [Why does it matter?]

**Team**: [Who will execute?]

**Commercialization**: [Path to market]

## Technical Approach (15 pages)

### 1. Significance and Background (2-3 pages)
**Problem Statement**:
- What problem are you solving?
- Why is it important?
- What are the limitations of current solutions?

**Market Need**:
- Market size and growth
- Customer pain points
- Regulatory/policy drivers

**Relevant Literature**:
- Prior art and research
- Gaps in current knowledge
- Your unique insight

### 2. Innovation (2-3 pages)
**What's New**:
- Technical innovation
- Novel approach or methodology
- Intellectual property

**Competitive Advantage**:
- vs. existing solutions
- Barriers to entry
- Defensibility

### 3. Technical Objectives (1-2 pages)
**Phase I Goals** (6-12 months):
- Objective 1: [Specific, measurable]
- Objective 2: [Specific, measurable]
- Objective 3: [Specific, measurable]

**Success Criteria**:
- How will you know you succeeded?
- Quantitative metrics
- Go/no-go decision points

### 4. Methods and Approach (5-7 pages)
**Task 1**: [Objective 1]
- Approach and methodology
- Expected outcomes
- Potential pitfalls
- Alternative approaches

**Task 2**: [Objective 2]
- [Same structure as Task 1]

**Task 3**: [Objective 3]
- [Same structure]

**Timeline**:
- Month 1-3: Task 1
- Month 4-6: Task 2  
- Month 7-9: Task 3
- Month 10-12: Integration and testing

### 5. Preliminary Data (1-2 pages)
**What We've Done So Far**:
- Proof-of-concept results
- Pilot studies
- Customer validation

**Figures and Charts**:
- Include key data
- Professional quality
- Clear labels and legends

### 6. Team and Resources (1-2 pages)
**Key Personnel**:
- Principal Investigator (PI): Background and relevant experience
- Co-Investigators: Complementary expertise
- Consultants/Advisors: Industry and technical experts

**Facilities and Equipment**:
- Lab space and equipment available
- Partnerships and collaborations

## Commercialization Plan (10 pages)

### 1. Market Opportunity (2-3 pages)
**Market Size**:
- TAM (Total Addressable Market)
- SAM (Serviceable Available Market)
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)

**Market Trends**:
- Growth drivers
- Technology adoption curves
- Regulatory tailwinds

**Customer Segments**:
- Primary target customers
- Use cases and applications

### 2. Competitive Landscape (2 pages)
**Direct Competitors**:
- Who are they?
- Their strengths and weaknesses
- Market share

**Indirect Competitors/Alternatives**:
- Current solutions customers use
- Why they're insufficient

**Your Advantage**:
- Differentiation
- Sustainable competitive moat

### 3. Business Model (1-2 pages)
**Revenue Model**:
- How will you make money?
- Pricing strategy
- Unit economics

**Go-to-Market**:
- Sales channels (direct, partners, online)
- Marketing strategy
- Customer acquisition cost

### 4. Development Plan (2-3 pages)
**Phase II Plans**:
- What will Phase II funding enable?
- 24-month development roadmap
- Key milestones

**Regulatory Path** (if applicable):
- FDA clearance/approval strategy
- Timeline and costs
- Regulatory consultants

**Manufacturing/Scale**:
- Path from prototype to production
- Supply chain considerations
- Cost reduction roadmap

### 5. Team and Funding (2 pages)
**Management Team**:
- CEO, CTO, key hires
- Industry experience
- Track record

**Advisors and Board**:
- Strategic advisors
- Technical advisors
- Board members

**Funding Strategy**:
- Capital raised to date
- Use of SBIR funds
- Future funding plans (VC, grants, revenue)

**Phase III Plans**:
- Government procurement opportunities
- Commercial sales
- Path to profitability

---

**Tips for Success**:
- Clear, concise writing (avoid jargon)
- Strong innovation story
- Credible commercialization plan
- Realistic timeline and budget
- Address reviewer concerns proactively
- Professional formatting and figures
- Proofread carefully (no typos!)

**Common Mistakes**:
- Overly ambitious objectives
- Weak commercialization section
- Insufficient preliminary data
- Budget doesn't match objectives
- Poor writing quality

**Timeline**: Budget 100-150 hours for a strong proposal
