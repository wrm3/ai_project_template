# VC Fundraising Best Practices

Best practices and rules for venture capital fundraising from pre-seed through Series A+.

## Core Principles

### 1. Preparation is Everything
- Start preparing 3-6 months before active fundraising
- Build comprehensive data room in advance
- Practice pitch 10+ times before first VC meeting
- Research every VC before outreach
- Have 6-9 months runway when starting fundraise

### 2. Storytelling Over Features
- Lead with problem, not solution
- Quantify pain points and market opportunity
- Show traction and momentum
- Connect metrics to story
- End with inspiring vision

### 3. Metrics Drive Decisions
- Know your key metrics cold
- Compare against industry benchmarks
- Show consistent growth trajectory
- Be honest about challenges
- Demonstrate path to next stage

### 4. Terms Matter More Than Valuation
- 1x non-participating liquidation preference (gold standard)
- Broad-based weighted average anti-dilution
- Reasonable board composition
- Limited protective provisions
- Avoid participating preferred and full ratchet

### 5. Choose Partners, Not Just Money
- Reference check VCs with portfolio founders
- Assess value-add beyond capital
- Evaluate decision-making style and speed
- Consider long-term partnership fit
- Prioritize helpful, transparent investors

## Fundraising Timeline Rules

### Pre-Fundraise (2-3 months before)
- Build pitch deck (15 slides)
- Create financial model (3-5 year projections)
- Organize data room
- Practice pitch with advisors
- Build VC target list (100+ firms)
- Identify warm intro paths

### Active Fundraising (2-4 months)
- Batch VC meetings (2-3 week sprints)
- Follow up within 24 hours of every meeting
- Respond to due diligence requests within 24-48 hours
- Keep all active VCs updated weekly
- Create urgency with parallel processes
- Don't let process drag past 6 months

### Due Diligence (4-6 weeks)
- Respond to requests promptly
- Be transparent about concerns
- Provide context for data
- Coordinate customer references
- Maintain momentum and urgency

### Closing (2-4 weeks)
- Finalize legal documentation
- Board and stockholder approvals
- Wire transfer confirmation
- Cap table update
- Public announcement coordination

## Pitch Deck Rules

### Content Requirements
- **15 slides maximum** (main deck)
- **One key message per slide**
- **Bullets, not paragraphs** (3-5 bullets max)
- **Quantify everything** (metrics, market size, impact)
- **Current data** (< 1 month old)
- **Traction over potential** (show, don't just tell)

### Slide Requirements

**Must Have**:
1. Cover (logo, tagline, contact)
2. Problem (clear, painful, quantified)
3. Solution (10x better, not 10%)
4. Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM with bottom-up)
5. Product (screenshots, demo)
6. Business Model (pricing, unit economics)
7. Traction (metrics, growth, customers)
8. Competition (2x2 matrix or table)
9. Competitive Advantage (moat)
10. Go-to-Market (channels, strategy)
11. Team (founders + key hires)
12. Financials (historical + projections)
13. Fundraising Ask (amount, use of funds, milestones)
14. Vision (long-term opportunity)
15. Thank You (contact, next steps)

**Appendix** (5-10 backup slides):
- Detailed financials
- Customer case studies
- Product roadmap
- Extended team bios
- Market research

### Design Rules
- Clean, professional design
- Consistent fonts (2-3 max) and colors
- High-resolution images (300 DPI+)
- Plenty of white space
- Large, readable font (24pt+ body text)
- High contrast (dark on light)

### Distribution Rules
- **PDF format** for email (password-protected)
- **PowerPoint/Keynote** for presenting
- **DocSend** for tracking analytics
- **Naming**: CompanyName_PitchDeck_Stage_Date.pdf
- **Password**: Simple (e.g., CompanySeed2025)
- **Version control**: Date every version

## VC Research and Outreach Rules

### Building Target List
- **100+ VCs minimum** for seed round
- **Filter by**: Stage, check size, industry, geography, thesis
- **Prioritize into tiers**: Tier 1 (perfect fit), Tier 2 (good), Tier 3 (possible), Tier 4 (stretch)
- **Research each VC**: Portfolio, recent investments, partner backgrounds, thesis

### Warm Introductions (Always Priority #1)
- **Leverage**: Portfolio founders, mutual investors, advisors, accelerators
- **Process**: Ask permission ‚Üí provide intro blurb ‚Üí thank connector
- **Success rate**: 30-50% meeting rate (vs. 5-10% cold)

### Cold Outreach
- **Personalize every email** (mention portfolio, thesis, recent investment)
- **Keep it short** (5-7 sentences max)
- **Lead with traction** or unique insight
- **Explain VC fit** specifically (not generic)
- **Clear CTA**: "Would you be open to a 15-min call?"
- **Follow-up cadence**: Day 4-5, Day 10-12, Day 20-25 (3 emails max)

### Email Subject Lines
- **Good**: "[Mutual Connection] intro - [Company]"
- **Good**: "[Company] - AI-powered revenue intelligence (Seed)"
- **Bad**: "Seeking investment"
- **Bad**: "Quick question"

## Meeting and Pitching Rules

### Before Meeting
- **Research VC** (1-2 hours): Partner bio, portfolio, recent investments, Twitter/LinkedIn
- **Prepare custom materials**: Standard deck + custom slides for thesis fit
- **Practice pitch**: 15-20 minutes (rehearse 5+ times)
- **Prepare questions**: 2-3 thoughtful questions for VC
- **Arrive early**: 5 minutes (in-person or Zoom)

### During Meeting
- **Time allocation**: 5 min intro ‚Üí 15-20 min pitch ‚Üí 15-20 min Q&A ‚Üí 5 min next steps
- **Start with problem** (hook them immediately)
- **Show, don't just tell** (product demo, metrics)
- **Listen carefully** to questions and concerns
- **Admit what you don't know** (honesty builds trust)
- **Gauge interest** level throughout
- **Clarify next steps** before ending

### After Meeting
- **Send thank you email** within 24 hours
- **Recap discussion** points and next steps
- **Provide requested materials** within 24-48 hours
- **Share updates** weekly if process is ongoing
- **Track in system**: Interest level, next steps, timeline

### Common Investor Questions (Be Prepared)

**Product**:
- How does your product work?
- What's your technical moat?
- What's on your roadmap?

**Market**:
- How big is the market?
- Why will you win vs. competitors?
- What are the trends driving growth?

**Traction**:
- What are your key metrics?
- What's your growth rate?
- Who are your customers?
- What's retention/churn?

**Business Model**:
- How do you make money?
- What are unit economics?
- What's CAC and LTV?
- What's gross margin?

**Team**:
- Why are you the right team?
- What are your backgrounds?
- Who are you hiring next?

**Fundraising**:
- How much are you raising?
- What's the valuation?
- Who else is investing?
- What are the milestones?

## Term Sheet Negotiation Rules

### Before Negotiating
- **Get multiple term sheets** if possible (leverage)
- **Consult startup lawyer** (worth the cost)
- **Understand market standards** (don't accept bad terms)
- **Prioritize terms** (partnership > control > economics > valuation)

### Negotiation Priorities (In Order)

**Priority 1 (Critical)**:
- **Partner and firm fit**: Can't change later (check references!)
- **Liquidation preference**: 1x non-participating ONLY
- **Board composition**: Balance control with value-add
- **Anti-dilution**: Broad-based weighted average (avoid full ratchet)

**Priority 2 (Important)**:
- **Protective provisions**: Limit to major events only
- **Valuation**: Fair market value (don't overprice)
- **Pro-rata rights**: Standard for lead investor
- **No-shop period**: 30 days preferred (45 max)

**Priority 3 (Nice-to-Have)**:
- **Option pool**: Post-money preferred (less founder dilution)
- **Drag-along rights**: Reasonable (minimum price, board approval)
- **Information rights**: Standard reporting (not excessive)

### Terms to Accept (Market Standard)
‚úÖ 1x non-participating liquidation preference
‚úÖ Broad-based weighted average anti-dilution
‚úÖ Pro-rata rights for lead investor
‚úÖ Standard protective provisions (sale, new equity, debt, dividends)
‚úÖ Drag-along with reasonable conditions
‚úÖ 30-45 day no-shop period

### Red Flags (Negotiate Hard or Walk Away)
üö© Participating preferred (2x money in exits)
üö© Multiple liquidation preference (2x, 3x, etc.)
üö© Full ratchet anti-dilution (punitive to founders)
üö© Excessive board control (investor dominates)
üö© Super voting rights (investor vetoes)
üö© Unreasonable no-shop (> 60 days)
üö© Unusual closing conditions (deal-killers)

### Negotiation Tactics
- **Be prepared to walk away** (have alternatives)
- **Focus on win-win** (long-term partnership)
- **Don't over-negotiate** minor points (pick battles)
- **Be transparent** about concerns
- **Seek founder advice** (those who've been there)
- **Remember**: You're choosing a partner for 5-10 years

## Due Diligence Rules

### Data Room Organization
Create folders with clear structure:
- Company Overview (formation docs, cap table, board resolutions)
- Financials (P&L, balance sheet, projections, revenue detail)
- Contracts (customers, vendors, partners, leases)
- IP (patents, trademarks, licenses, assignments)
- Legal (litigation, compliance, insurance, tax)
- Product (docs, architecture, roadmap, metrics)
- Sales & Marketing (pipeline, materials, strategy)
- HR (org chart, compensation, employee agreements)

### Response Time Rules
- **Urgent requests** (contracts, financials): 24 hours
- **Standard requests** (additional docs): 24-48 hours
- **Complex requests** (analysis, modeling): 2-3 days
- **Always acknowledge**: Email receipt within 4 hours

### Transparency Rules
- **Be honest** about challenges and concerns
- **Provide context** for any red flags
- **Don't hide** issues (they'll find out eventually)
- **Explain** changes or anomalies in data
- **Proactively address** potential concerns

### Customer Reference Rules
- **Select 3-5 diverse customers**: Different sizes, industries, use cases
- **Prepare references**: Brief on VC, provide talking points
- **Key topics**: Why they chose you, implementation experience, results/ROI, product feedback, future plans
- **Thank them**: Always follow up with gratitude

### Momentum Maintenance
- **Weekly updates** to VC (even if no progress)
- **Proactive communication** on delays or issues
- **Keep process moving** (don't let stall)
- **Escalate** if VC is slow (talk to partner)
- **Set deadlines** for closing (create urgency)

## Cap Table and Dilution Rules

### Option Pool Sizing
- **Pre-seed**: 10-15% of fully diluted
- **Seed**: 10-15% (refresh if depleted)
- **Series A**: 10-15% (refresh)
- **Series B+**: 8-12% (refresh)

### Pool Timing Strategy
- **Pre-money pool**: Investor-friendly (dilutes founders before investment)
- **Post-money pool**: Founder-friendly (dilutes everyone including investor)
- **Negotiate**: Push for post-money or smaller pre-money pool

### Dilution Expectations
- **Seed round**: 15-25% dilution
- **Series A**: 20-30% dilution
- **Series B**: 15-25% dilution
- **Series C+**: 10-20% dilution

### Founder Ownership Over Time
- **After seed**: 70-80%
- **After Series A**: 50-60%
- **After Series B**: 35-50%
- **After Series C+**: 25-40%
- **At IPO/exit**: 15-30% (if successful)

### Modeling Dilution
Always model these scenarios:
- Current ownership breakdown
- Post-round ownership (with option pool)
- Next round dilution (assume 20-25%)
- Exit scenarios ($25M, $50M, $100M, $250M, $500M)
- Founder proceeds in each scenario

### Cap Table Tools
- **Early stage (free)**: Google Sheets, Carta free tier
- **Growth stage (paid)**: Carta ($1,200-$6,000/year), Pulley ($500-$2,000/year)
- **Features needed**: Modeling, 409A valuations, employee equity management, investor reporting

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Strategic Mistakes
‚ùå Raising too early (weak traction)
‚ùå Raising too late (< 6 months runway)
‚ùå Raising too much (high valuation pressure)
‚ùå Raising too little (not enough runway)
‚ùå Wrong investors (misaligned values)
‚ùå Ignoring terms (bad deal structure)
‚ùå No alternatives (single term sheet)
‚ùå Taking too long (> 6 months)

### Pitch Mistakes
‚ùå Too much text (use bullets, not paragraphs)
‚ùå Weak problem (not painful enough)
‚ùå No traction (vanity metrics only)
‚ùå Ignoring competition (claiming "no competitors")
‚ùå Unrealistic projections (10x growth with no basis)
‚ùå Weak team (missing key roles)
‚ùå Vague use of funds (no detail)
‚ùå No clear ask (missing amount or terms)

### Negotiation Mistakes
‚ùå Over-negotiating (fighting every term)
‚ùå Under-negotiating (accepting bad terms)
‚ùå Valuation obsession (ignoring terms)
‚ùå No lawyer (trying to DIY)
‚ùå Playing investors against each other (burns bridges)
‚ùå Rushing (pressure to close quickly)

### Due Diligence Mistakes
‚ùå Slow responses (> 48 hours)
‚ùå Disorganized data room
‚ùå Hiding issues (lack of transparency)
‚ùå No customer references ready
‚ùå Losing momentum (process stalls)

## Key Metrics to Track

### Fundraising Efficiency
- **VCs contacted**: Target 100+ for seed
- **Meeting conversion**: 15-25% (warm), 5-10% (cold)
- **First to second meeting**: 40-50%
- **Partner meeting rate**: 20-30% of second meetings
- **Term sheet rate**: 10-15% of partner meetings
- **Time to close**: 3-6 months typical

### Process Velocity
- **Time to first meeting**: 1-2 weeks from intro
- **Time to second meeting**: 1-2 weeks from first
- **Time to term sheet**: 2-4 weeks from partner meeting
- **Time to close**: 6-8 weeks from term sheet

### Success Indicators
- Multiple term sheets (2-3 ideal)
- Competitive fundraising environment
- Founder-friendly terms achieved
- Closed on timeline (3-6 months)
- Raised target amount
- Fair market valuation
- Strong investor partnership
